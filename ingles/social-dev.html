Social Development

Responsabilidad Social

In Calima Farms, we firmly believe that the most successful agribusinesses are those that cultivate social responsibility and shared value within their corporate practices. That is why, we have launched a series of initiatives meant to empower the communities where we work. Through an inclusive social vision and concrete actions, we are improving quality of life of our fruit producers and packing house employees.

Rural Responsibility

Economic Prosperity

In Colombia, as in other emerging economies, farmers receive a nominal faction of the costs paid by international fruit consumers. To change this unfair equation, Calima Farms works to cut intermediaries in the fruit supply chain. By buying directly from farmers associations and independent growers, we guarantee them a fairer price for the fruit they have worked so hard to produce. Unlike other fruit wholesalers and exporters in Colombia, who can take up to 90 days to pay producers for their harvest, Calima Farms works proactively to ensure that farmers receive their payment in advance or within two weeks after the fruit is collected on the farm.

Product Quality

Calima Farms sources fruits exclusively from farmers who have been certified in Good Agricultural Practices (BPA) by the Colombian Institute of Agriculture (ICA). As part of our vision of social value with small and medium producers, we are committed with improving the production, management and quality of our fruit suppliers. Our firm has established productive partnerships with numerous producer associations and with international partners in order to 1) increase the number of local producers with Global GAP certification, 2) reduce post-harvest losses, and, consequently, 3) strengthen the quality and the phytosanitary controls of our farmers.

Crops for Peace

Colombia's countryside is blessed with fertile soils and diverse microclimates that make it a perfect paradise for the cultivation of various agri-foods. The rural areas, however, have also suffered disproportionately from the displacement, violence and illicit economies associated with the five decades-long armed conflict. Calima Farms is conscious that to achieve a lasting peace, we need to work in partnership with local and global stakeholders to rebuild a rural economy that generates shared value for Colombia’s war-torn farming communities. With this objective in mind, our firm prioritizes the purchase of fruit directly from producers in the municipalities designated as ZOMACS ("zones most affected by the armed conflict"). Thanks to these efforts, our agribusiness is working alongside rural communities to cultivate legitimate value chains that foster opportunities and peace.

Workforce Development

Gender Empowerment

In rural Colombia, female head of households face many cultural and systemic challenges to enter the workforce. At Calima Farms, we believe that rural working women are the backbone of our agribusiness, and that investing in their future is a powerful way to improve gender equality and alleviate the levels of poverty that ail Colombia’s countryside. Therefore, we have committed ourselves to prioritizing the hiring of single mothers and middle-age women at our fruit packing plant. By providing them with meaningful employment opportunities and fair wages, we are investing in their ability to improve the wellbeing of themselves, their families, and the rural communities that they represent.

Skills for Rural Youth

The lack of rural employment forces many young adults to leave behind their communities in search of better opportunities in the cities. To counter this trend in rural-to-urban migration, we are hiring and building up the skills of young people from across La Unión and neighboring municipalities. In the coming months, we will also be rolling out a paid, internship program that will serve as a hiring pipeline for local youth interested in remaining in their communities and pursuing a career in the fruit packing and export sector.
